Key Findings:
Analyzing Inter-Federative Governance in Policy Implementation

Renata | Aug 11, 2023

Let’s review the key findings from my master's degree thesis, Analyzing Inter-Federative Governance in Policy Implementation: The Cases of Primary Healthcare and Basic Education in Brazil.

Context: The Brazilian Education and Health Systems

This section delves into the interfederative governance of Brazil's informal education system and formal health system, from 1988 til now.

Education: proposing collaboration

The CF/88 established shared responsibilities among federal, state, and local governments. This has led to many challenges, many related to policy overlaps, heterogeneity, and insufficient coordination between federative entities in formulating and implementing educational policies. There have been many attempts to instate a national education system, but so far none have been officially implemented.

Health: formalizing collaboration

Unlike education, health has a well-defined governance process that formalizes collaboration between different levels of government, aiming to ensure equal access to healthcare services across the country: the Unified Health System (SUS), introduced in 1990. However, it still has its share of challenges, especially as it attempts to move from a logic of municipalization to regionalization.

    Insight and added value

  • There is a need for understanding federalism beyond territorialism, considering legal frameworks and stakeholders' perspectives.
  • The thesis builds on the existing literature by offering an analysis of the existing legal framework of the two public service systems as well as their practical implementation.


    • Research Methods

    This section presents the thesis' hypothesis, units of analysis and logic.

    Hypothesis

      IF

      An inter-federative collaboration regime is formally defined and legalized to assist in a country's policy implementation,

      THEN

      the different levels of federal power work together more efficiently and effectively to achieve it,

      BECAUSE

      there is a clear and institutionalized governance process enabling them to do so.


    Units of Analysis

    Logic

    The study employs a qualitative approach, beginning with a language analysis of the legal framework in the health and education systems to understand structural differences in inter-federative collaborations using the software R. Subsequently, it adopts an ethnographic approach, using semi-structured interviews with experts in health and education to investigate the levels of inter-federative governance in practice.


    The main methods used were:

    De Jure: An Observational Study
  • Using software R, seven different law instruments were systematically analyzed to understand how the language used in education and health systems are proponing collaboration between inter-federative levels
    • De Facto: An Ethnographical Study
  • Semi-structured interviews with public healthcare and public education experts to understand in practice the efficacy and efficiency in the collaboration between inter-federative levels.

    • Key Findings

    The thesis investigates public governance coordination in Brazil's federalism, focusing on healthcare and education.

    • Healthcare: The Unified Health System (SUS) introduced in 1990 has a well-defined governance process but faces challenges in regionalization.
    • Education: Many attempts to instate a national education system have been made, but none have been officially implemented.

    Challenges and Opportunities

    • Healthcare: Challenges include moving from municipalization to regionalization.
    • Education: Challenges include unclear responsibilities, policy overlaps, heterogeneity, and insufficient coordination.

    Conclusions

    The thesis provides valuable insights into public governance coordination processes in Brazil, focusing on healthcare and education. Collaboration between different levels of government is essential, and the study offers a complex systems viewpoint.
